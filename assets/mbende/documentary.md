# Text
Mbende is a popular dance style
Practised by the Zezuru of Murehwa

Characterised by acrobatic and sensual movements
The point is for the encounter to feel good

The dance does have a weird name
Before colonialism, it was named Mbende
Wrapped up as "fertility"
Pleasure costs labour

Colonial Social Pressure forced the change 
of the name to Jerusarema (Jerusalem)
To remove any sexual connotations

Couples take turns to dance in the centre,
With men expected to make a grand entrance
A front flip is a strict requirement for flair

Men often crouch,whilst jerking their arms
Vigorously kicking the ground with the right leg
An immitation of a burrowing mole

Played by a polyrythmic single drummer with three drums,
accompanied by men playing woodblock clappers and triangles,
and by women clapping and yodelling

Mbende does not rely on intricate footwork, stamping or band of drummers
And no songs or lyrics are involved
It just has to be an encounter sufficient to make one feel good.
