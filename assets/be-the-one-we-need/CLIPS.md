# Asset slots — be-the-one-we-need

Drop source clips here. Filenames must match exactly.
Accepts .mp4 or .gif — if using GIFs, rename to .mp4 or adjust the src paths in BeTheOneWeNeed.tsx.

| File | Act | Content | Duration needed | Notes |
|------|-----|---------|----------------|-------|
| `mirror.mp4` | I | Person looking in a mirror, face obscured or out-of-frame | ≥ 3s | The reflection is the subject. Face not visible = "are you what you are?" has no answer in the frame. |
| `shadow.mp4` | I | Person's shadow on pavement, body not in frame | ≥ 3s | Representation without presence. Shadow = resting cut. No body = no point-identity. |
| `crowd.mp4` | I | Crowd from directly above, individuals indistinct | ≥ 3s | The question "which one are you?" has no answer at this resolution. Bird's-eye removes the receiver's individuation. |
| `hands.mp4` | II | Two hands reaching toward each other, not touching | ≥ 3s | No text in this window. The gap is the theorem. Avoid clichéd "almost touching" — ambiguity is better than pathos. |
| `static.mp4` | III | Analogue TV static / signal interference | ≥ 3s | The transmission exists; the signal does not decode. Pure form, no content. |
| `archive.mp4` | III | Old letters, faded photographs, documents in a foreign script | ≥ 3s | "What you understand" — the archive is legible but meaning is bounded by the reader's graph. |
| `lip.mp4` | IV | Extreme close-up of a mouth speaking — no audio, no subtitles | ≥ 2s | Silent. The utterance is complete; nothing arrives. This is `thm:unextractable` made visual. |
| `ocean.mp4` | IV | Ocean surface from directly above, depth invisible | ≥ 2s | The depth is there. The surface tells you nothing about it. Meaning is region-valued and unextractable. |
| `babel.mp4` | V | City timelapse or crowd scene with visible signage in multiple languages | ≥ 3s | The diagnosis. Many meanings, no shared floor. |
| `light.mp4` | V | Single beam of light through a keyhole or narrow gap into darkness | ≥ 3s | **Full colour — the only colour moment in the entire ad.** The product is the gap that lets meaning through. |

## Visual grammar rules

- **B&W**: Acts I–III and babel (Act V diagnosis). Every clip in B&W except the resolution.
- **Desaturated but not B&W**: lip and ocean (Act IV). Colour begins returning, not yet arrived.
- **Full colour**: light only (final clip). The break is load-bearing — if light.mp4 is too dark or monochrome, the colour return won't read. Choose a clip with strong warm or blue colour.

## Timing notes

- The hands clip (9s–12s) carries no text. Resist the urge to add any. The silence is structural.
- The lip clip (18s–20s) has no audio. If the source has audio, mute it. The viewer should not hear the words.
- "others can only understand" and "what you understand" must be felt as one sentence split across two clips. The 3s gap between 14.8s and 15.2s is the comma. Don't trim it.
- The keyhole light clip must have the beam visible in the first second — the text appears at 25.5s and the clip ends at 28s.

## Suggested sources

- Mirror: Pexels "reflection mirror" — search for abstract/obscured faces
- Shadow: Unsplash/Pexels street photography from above
- Crowd: Getty aerial crowd footage, or DJI drone stock
- Hands: many options; avoid the Michelangelo reference — too loaded
- Static: Pixabay has free TV static loops
- Archive: old letter texture, or footage of someone reading handwritten script in an unfamiliar language
- Lip: stock close-up of speaking mouth — common in Pexels/Unsplash
- Ocean: drone footage of calm sea surface, looking straight down
- Babel: any dense multilingual city street; Tokyo or Istanbul work well
- Light: keyhole, gap in curtains, torch in cave — strong directional beam essential
